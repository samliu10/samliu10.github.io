LEICA M6

Design | Vintage Camera | June 2020

Maybe it's the way the buttons look or how the film develops, but no matter what it is, there's just something so nostalgic about vintage cameras.

The inspiration behind this design is the Leica M6 film camera produced from 1984 to 2002. Initial designs feature only the basic components of the camera such as the body, viewfinder, lens, and a few buttons, but the final design ties together all of the subtleties in these different parts. The vintage feel is contrasted with a more modern flat design to keep the focus on the simplicity of the camera because sometimes it's true, less is more.

Sam Liu
