CalmTrails

Mobile App | Case Study | Fall 2020

"This trail is too crowded. I donâ€™t feel safe out on this trail alone. I just want to get away from the stress of everyday life." These were common frustrations that came up in user interviews with college students who run and/or hike alone. Going for a run or hike is a way to de-stress and have some alone time, but trails can be too crowded. In addition, encounters with some strangers or parts of the trail may make people feel unsafe alone.

CalmTrails is the result of a semester-long case study for a human-computer interaction course that began with user interviews and ended with a high-fidelity prototype. It is a mobile app that aims to address four aspects of being outdoors: improve the recreational experience for college students who hike or run outdoors alone, support emotional and mental wellbeing and desire to de-stress, encourage goal-setting and progress tracking, and provide measures to ensure personal outdoor safety.

Users have access to AR functionality that generates a map based on a scan of their surroundings on which they can see their location and places nearby as well as indicate locations of an hazards on the trail. After a trip, users are given the option to reflect on their trip and see their progress towards any long-term goals they set, e.g. distance goals, frequency goals. The app also generates trail recommendations for them based on these goals. Lastly, users can input emergency contacts that can view their location while they are on the trail as a personal safety measure.
