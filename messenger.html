Facebook Messenger

Web App | Case Study | April 2020

If you ask a typical college student what messaging platform they use, chances are they'll say Facebook Messenger. This app lets you easily create chats with other people, connecting you with friends, classmates, and organizations.

But on the other hand, your messaging history can quickly become cluttered with old, spammy, and irrelevant chats. There is currently no way to delete or hide multiple chats at the same time. Instead, web app users have to manually click on each chat, hit delete, and then do it all over again. That doesn't seem bad at all when it's only one chat, but imagine trying to clear out months or even years of unwanted chats. And it's even more of a hassle on the mobile app — users must swipe left on each individual chat to show the delete option, hit delete, and then confirm the action. What a pain.

This new feature gives users the ability to efficiently remove multiple unwanted chats at the same time. Select chats by clicking the circle next to each chat photo, and then hide or delete them, actions currently supported by both the web and mobile app. Cancel any selections by hitting the cancel option at the top. Simple, right?
