Cavern of Gold

Java

Date: November 2019


According to all good adventure movies, the path to the treasure is not an easy one. In this simulation, the character is placed on a map and uses a locally-optimized traversal algorithm to get to the treasure. Discovery of the treasure causes a change in the map, and all of the previously hidden gold is revealed. The character collects as much gold as possible before heading to the exit.

However, there's a catch. The character must escape the maze without taking more than a certain number of steps. If the character succeeds, the character's score is increased by a bonus multiplier dependent on the amount of gold collected. Otherwise, the character fails, and they receive a score of 0.

This implementation relies on a globally-optimized traversal algorithm to maximize the amount of gold collected by ranking gold-to-distance ratios of tiles in the map. The character always escapes in time, and the mission is a success.
